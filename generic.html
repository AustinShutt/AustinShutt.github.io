About Me!

I find myself drawn most to performance and efficiency in software. I am constantly reading books that improve my understanding of how software interacts with the hardware and how one can write high level code using a language such as C++ and have the compiler assist in turning that high level code in to code that can run well in the target environment.

Overall application architecture and best team practices have been my focus lately as I explore the benefits of continuous delivery models and test driven software. I am currently enrolled to receive a computer science degree, however curiosity drives my education much more than any assignment ever could. I have invited a friend to work on a project collaboratively to be able to actively practice some of the industry best practices that I have been reading about when it comes to group coding.

Employment History

I currently do freelance software development running a Fiverr storefront in which I produce small coding projects for customers on the site. This has allowed me to gain a great knowledge base of a select few languages and exposure to a number of diverse problem sets. Maybe more importantly it has taught me to speak to an end user about software purpose, design, and customer satisfaction when working on a project.

I am a Navy Veteran and had a fantastic ~7 years in the U.S. Navy as a Search and Rescue Medical Techician flying in the MH-60S performing high altitude aerial rescue in the Pacific Northwest. I flew over 165 live missions and ~1500 flight hours. I was eventually in an enlisted leadership position in which I directly trained and equipped well over 15 aircrew in rappel/hoist operations and medical evaluations at a world class level. During my time there I produced a couple videos of what our experience was like.
